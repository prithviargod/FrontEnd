Welcome to our interactive guide to the planets in our solar system. Below, you'll find information about each planet.

1. Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and has a thin atmosphere. It is named after the Roman messenger god.

2. Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is known for its thick, toxic atmosphere. It is often called the "Evening Star" or "Morning Star."

3. Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known celestial body to support life. It has a diverse ecosystem and is home to millions of species. 

Did you know? Earth is the only planet not named after a Roman or Greek god.

4. Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often referred to as the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance. It has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system. 

Fun Fact: Mars has the tallest volcano in our solar system, Olympus Mons!

5. Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and is known for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm. It has a strong magnetic field. 

Fun Fact: Jupiter has more than 79 moons, including the largest one, Ganymede.

6. Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system, which consists of icy particles. It is the sixth planet from the Sun. 

Did you know? Saturn's rings are made up of ice particles and rocky debris.

7. Uranus

Uranus is a gas giant and is unique in that it rotates on its side. It is the seventh planet from the Sun. 

Fun Fact: Uranus is the only planet in our solar system that rolls on its side as it orbits the Sun.

8. Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun. 

Did you know? Neptune has the strongest winds in our solar system, reaching speeds of over 1,500 miles per hour!



Explore the wonders of our solar system and continue to be curious about the universe!
